# It was the day after New Years, where a very hungover high school junior, while throwing up over the side of a boat in Cabo
# thought of what could be done with one of our nations most respected figures in the presidency.
# I thought of the ways we could show moral goodness triumphing over the supreme evil that is Nazism, the prisms to project
# the good of the world through the office of the presidency, and considering what Glenn was known for, there was only one answer:
# Mars.
# The Mars landing, although it has been memed to death and back, to me represents the moral triumph over fascism that the world of TNO craves.
# It's high minded science, backed by a President who is the antithesis of Hitler, not just claiming humanities future lies in freedom, but showing the world the German menace can, and will be, beaten.
# It was a message that took me months to impliment, hours spent on the weekends coding line after line of mission types, researchable objects, and fundraising mechanics, on top of being in a musical and school.
# And it was time I would never trade back if I had the chance.
# TNO as a whole has been the best thing to ever happen to me, giving me purpose and drive when none existed before, and providing me a way to stand out among my peers as I enter into the world of college admissions.
# And the best thing in TNO for me has been Glenn.
# It was my opportunity to have full control not only over design, but the story and message I wanted to tell, the story I felt I needed to tell, and one, for all its faults, bugs, and critiscm, I am extremely happy with.
# So I leave this message hidden in the confines of my proudest creation, made from hours of blood sweat and tears, to leave my final imprint on a project that's meant so much.
